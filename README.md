# Hr-Attrition
Project Overview

The HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard is a Tableau-based HR Analytics project designed to analyze employee attrition patterns, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, and department-level trends.

This dashboard helps HR teams and business stakeholders understand why employees leave, identify high-risk attrition groups, and make data-driven workforce planning and retention decisions.

Project Objective

The main objective of this project is to analyze employee attrition and provide actionable HR insights through interactive visualizations.

This dashboard helps to:
Analyze attrition across age groups, gender, departments, and education fields
Evaluate job satisfaction across different job roles
Identify high-risk areas contributing to employee turnover
Support employee retention strategies
Enable strategic HR decision-making
Problem Statement

HR teams often struggle to understand workforce trends when employee data is scattered across multiple sources and stored in raw tabular format.

Without proper visualization, it becomes difficult to:

Identify which age groups have the highest attrition
Detect which departments or job roles are vulnerable
Analyze employee satisfaction levels
Understand demographic distribution patterns
Monitor overall retention performance

This project transforms raw HR data into a clear, interactive Tableau dashboard for easier interpretation and business use.

 Dashboard Components

The dashboard contains multiple interactive visualizations to explore attrition and workforce patterns.

 Age Band Pie Charts

Shows employee distribution across different age groups.

 Gender-wise Attrition Pie Chart

Displays employee exits based on gender.

 Department-wise Attrition View

Highlights which departments are experiencing the highest employee turnover.

 Education Field & Attrition Bar Chart

Shows which educational backgrounds are associated with higher attrition.

 Job Role vs Job Satisfaction Highlight Table

Helps identify job roles with lower satisfaction levels and potential attrition risk.

 Employee Age Distribution Histogram

Visualizes the age structure of the workforce.

 Active vs Attrition Employee Count

Provides an overview of employee retention and resignation counts.

 Key Insights & Findings

Some major findings from the dashboard include:

Most employees fall within the 25–44 age group
Male attrition is higher compared to female attrition
Certain departments such as Sales or R&D show higher employee turnover
Job satisfaction varies across roles, indicating possible areas for HR intervention
Attrition trends can be linked to workforce demographics and job satisfaction levels

These insights can help HR teams focus on retention strategies, employee engagement, and workforce planning.

By transforming raw HR data into meaningful insights, this dashboard becomes a valuable tool for people analytics and organizational planning.
